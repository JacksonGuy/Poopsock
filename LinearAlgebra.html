Chapter 1: Matrices

If we take a linear equation, say \(2x-4=4\), that equation has a particular solution, \(x=4\). In this instance, our equation has a single, solution solution, since our
equation is of a single variable.

In this chapter, we will be looking at systems of linear equations, where we consider the solutions of several linear equations at once. For example, consider the system $$ 2x + 3y - 3z = 4 \\ -x + 2y + z = 0 \\ 4x + 8z = 12 $$ This system contains 3 linear equations, each having 3 variables.
